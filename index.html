Project Idea

Overview

The project is to create an all-in-one smartphone app for teletherapy by using the MIT App Inventor or other app development software. This app will function like a dating app but for patients and their potential therapists. It also includes other functions like self-monitoring dashboards and educational/motivational content. The target audience is anyone who may be seeking alternatives to in person therapy.

Motivation

This project idea is a response to the lack of accessibility to therapy (physically and financially) and the difficulty of finding a therapist that is most suited to an individual’s needs and preferences. Individuals that are seeking therapy have to pay exorbitant prices for at least a few therapy sessions before getting a sense of whether the therapist is best suited for them.

Description

Therapists that sign up to this app will include their pricing, schedule, area of specialisation, experience and some personal. Patient’s will then be able to swipe right on the therapists they want to engage with. However, patients need not include information on their end, patients will only reveal information while they are talking to the therapist. The patient will be able to ask the therapist several questions via chat before deciding on which therapist to go with.

As for the actual teletherapy, it will be done via voice and video calling. Patients and their therapist will schedule sessions according to their availabilities.

Educational content will also be published related to mental health.

Self-monitoring dashboards will be implemented. This engages users to have an option to track their mood, symptoms and habits. Users can choose what they wish to monitor or as per advised by their therapist.

Notifications will also be implemented in order to consistently engage users to ensure progress. These notifications will include appointment reminders, logging of mood, symptoms and habits, new educational content and daily motivational/mental health

A security password will also be implemented and optional for users. Since conversations, calls and tracking of mood, symptoms will be documented in the app, the user will have the option to set up a password to log in every time.
